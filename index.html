Birds

Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterized by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, and a high metabolic rate.

There are over 10,000 known living species of birds, divided into two subclasses: the Palaeognathae (including the flightless ratites) and the Neognathae (all other birds).

Types of Birds

Some common types of birds include:

  • Peacocks: Peacocks are large, colorful birds that are known for their stunning feathers and beautiful mating dances.
  • Eagles: Eagles are large birds of prey that are known for their sharp talons and keen eyesight.
  • Penguins: Penguins are flightless birds that are native to the southern hemisphere. They are known for their black and white feathers and their ability to swim underwater.
